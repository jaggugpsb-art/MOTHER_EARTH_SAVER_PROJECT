# Theme & Problem Statement — WattWise AI

**Theme:** Clean & Green Technology — Energy Management

## Problem

Household electricity use is difficult to see and even harder to manage before a monthly bill arrives. In India, demand commonly peaks in the evening when people return home and switch on cooling, cooking, lighting, laundry, and charging. That concentrated demand puts pressure on the grid and often requires more carbon-intensive generation. Many homes also run flexible appliances—washing machines, dishwashers, and electric-vehicle chargers—at peak time simply because there is no simple, personalised way to identify a better window.

This affects households through higher bills, communities through stressed local electricity infrastructure, and the environment through higher peak-time emissions. A small behaviour change across many homes can matter: moving flexible loads away from the evening peak reduces the need for additional grid generation without asking families to give up essential comfort.

## Why existing solutions fall short

Smart-meter dashboards typically show past consumption, not a useful plan for tomorrow. Generic conservation tips such as “switch off unused appliances” are well-intentioned but do not consider a home’s size, number of occupants, weather, work-from-home activity, or which appliances can realistically be moved. Full smart-home systems can automate schedules, but require costly connected devices and are inaccessible to many renters and middle-income households.

## Our approach

WattWise AI is a lightweight, privacy-friendly decision tool that works in any browser. A household enters a few conditions for tomorrow: home size, occupancy, temperature, work-from-home activity, and flexible appliances. An interpretable regression model forecasts expected energy demand, estimates its carbon impact, and identifies the amount of flexible energy that can move from the 6–9 pm peak to a lower-demand late-night period.

The output is intentionally practical: an estimated kWh forecast, carbon estimate, and one clear personalised action. The model runs locally, so it is instant, free to deploy, and does not collect household data. This makes energy intelligence available without a smart meter, an app download, or an expensive hardware ecosystem.

## Expected impact

For an individual household, WattWise aims to identify roughly 10–25% lower peak impact when flexible loads are available. At a community scale, similar decisions reduce coincident evening demand and encourage lower-carbon electricity use. The solution demonstrates that AI can support economic savings and environmental outcomes at the same time.
